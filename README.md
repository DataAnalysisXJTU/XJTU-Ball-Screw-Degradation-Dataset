# XJTU-Ball-Screw-Degradation-Dataset
A public dataset for ball screw performance degradation and lifetime testing, collected and maintained by Xi'an Jiaotong University (XJTU) The key hardware parameters of the tested ball screw are as follows: the lead is 8 mm, the accuracy grade is P2, the rated dynamic load is 16,400 N, and the preload is 1,500 N. Throughout the experiment, the external load was kept constant at 1,000 N.

A total of 220 performance degradation tests were conducted in this study, with each test lasting 7 hours. A variable-speed loading scheme was adopted to simulate performance degradation under multiple operating conditions. During the first 20 tests, the rotational speed was set to 300 r/min. Thereafter, the speed was increased by 200 r/min after every 20 tests, so as to cover the degradation behavior of the ball screw under different rotational speeds.

The friction torque was measured once every five degradation tests after disassembly. Meanwhile, the current signal was also measured at a measurement speed of 100 r/min, with a current sampling frequency of 10 kHz. The friction torque was calculated as the mean value over the travel stroke.
